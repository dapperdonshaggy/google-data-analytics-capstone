Ask

The project delineates the inquiries posed to the Cyclystics data and establishes the context in which these questions are explored below.

As a junior data analyst within the marketing analyst team at Cyclistic, a bike-share company in Chicago, your role is crucial. The marketing director holds the belief that the future success of the company hinges on optimizing the number of annual memberships. Consequently, your team aims to gain a comprehensive understanding of the distinct usage patterns of Cyclistic bikes by casual riders and annual members. Armed with these insights, your team intends to formulate a new marketing strategy aimed at enticing casual riders to transition into becoming annual members. However, the approval of these recommendations by Cyclistic executives is contingent on robust data insights and professional data visualizations that substantiate your proposals.

1.How do annual members and casual riders use Cyclistic bikes differently? 2.Why would casual riders buy Cyclistic annual memberships? 3.How can Cyclistic use digital media to influence casual riders to become members?

Data Cleaning

Eliminated Rows With Missing Values

As part of my assumptions, I considered any trip lacking a start or end station as an error, prompting the removal of such entries from the dataset. Additionally, I identified and removed blank rows associated with coordinates to ensure data integrity.

#dropping N/A trips
no_blank_trips <- drop_na(tripdata)
# Calculate trip duration in seconds
no_blank_trips$trip_duration <- as.numeric(difftime(no_blank_trips$ended_at, no_blank_trips$started_at, units = "min"))

# Rounding trip durations to 2 decimal places
no_blank_trips$trip_duration <- round(no_blank_trips$trip_duration, digits = 2)
# Filtering completed trips based on duration
completed_trips <- no_blank_trips[(no_blank_trips$trip_duration > 1 & no_blank_trips$trip_duration < 34560),]

# Using completed_trips as trips_summary
trips_summary <- completed_trips

Act

The final phase of the data analysis process involves implementing actions based on the insights derived from the analysis. In this context, actionable steps have been formulated in the form of recommendations for the Cyclistic team, stemming from the initial project questions outlined below.

Questions and Recommendations:

1. How do annual members and casual riders use Cyclistic bikes differently?

  1. On average, Casual Members tend to opt for longer rides on Saturdays, while Annual members show a preference for Thursdays.

  2. Casual Members exhibit a preference for nighttime rides, whereas Annual Members favor mornings.

  3. Both Casual and Annual Members favor classic bikes, though Casual Members seem more inclined to experiment with electric bikes, indicating a less pronounced preference compared to Annual Members.

2. Why would casual riders buy Cyclistic annual memberships?

  • While the provided data might not comprehensively address this question, potential benefits influencing casual riders to switch to Annual Memberships could include:

    1. Unlimited rides within a 24-hour period.

    2. Discounted ride rates based on peak periods such as months, days, or specific times of the day.

3. How can Cyclistic use digital media to influence casual riders to become members?

  1. Digital Surveys: Implement surveys at the end of each ride to gather qualitative data on bicycle choices and trip purposes. Offer casual riders a glimpse of potential savings with an Annual Membership and provide a discount code for their initial year.

  2. Targeted Digital Media Campaigns: Leverage insights from the analysis, focusing on peak periods for casual riders. Offer discounted Annual Memberships during these peak periods to attract attention and encourage the switch.

  3. Referral Campaigns: Target existing Annual Members with referral incentives. If an existing member persuades a casual rider to switch:

    • The existing member receives a percentage discount on renewal for each converted casual rider.

    • The casual rider gets a discount on their new Annual Membership at sign-up.

These recommendations aim to capitalize on identified usage patterns and preferences, providing a strategic approach for Cyclistic to enhance user engagement and promote the transition from casual to annual memberships.
